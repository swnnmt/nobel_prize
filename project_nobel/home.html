    Nobel Prize in Physics 1936

    Discovery of the positron


    By studying the tracks of cosmic ray particles in a cloud chamber, Carl D. Anderson discovered a positively-charged particle with a mass seemingly equal to an electron’s. In 1932, Anderson’s particle was the first antiparticle proven by experiment and was named a “positron”. Watch Anderson explaining what a positron is.
